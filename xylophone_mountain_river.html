Empowered Women

Serena Williams

Serena Williams is an American professional tennis player who has won 23 Grand Slam singles titles, the most by any player in the Open Era. She is also a four-time Olympic gold medalist. Throughout her career, she has maintained her mental and physical strength, making her one of the most successful athletes of all time.

Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She is known for speaking out for the right to education for girls in the Swat Valley region of Pakistan, where the local Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school. Her bravery and tenacity earned Malala the Nobel Peace Prize in 2014.

Greta Thunberg

Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist who has gained recognition for inspiring and leading a global movement for climate change. She began her activism in August 2018 when she staged a week-long protest outside the Swedish parliament, and has since grown her movement into a global phenomenon.

Shirin Ebadi

Shirin Ebadi is an Iranian lawyer, human rights activist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. She has devoted her life to promoting and protecting the rights of women and children in Iran and has been an outspoken critic of the Islamic regime. In 2003, she became the first Iranian and the first Muslim woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize for her leadership in the struggle for democracy and human rights.

Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Ruth Bader Ginsburg was an American lawyer and a Supreme Court Justice. She was the second woman to serve on the Supreme Court and the first Jewish woman and first woman of color. Throughout her career, she worked to create legal protection for equal rights and access to justice for all genders.

Ellen Johnson Sirleaf

Ellen Johnson Sirleaf is a Liberian politician who served as president of Liberia from 2006 to 2018. She is the first elected female head of state in Africa and was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 2011 for her non-violent struggle for the safety of women and for women’s rights to full participation in peace-building work.

Oprah Winfrey

Oprah Winfrey is an American talk show host, media executive, and philanthropist. She is best known for her self-titled talk show, which ran from 1986 to 2011. In addition to her television career, she has used her success to empower and advocate for women around the world.

Michelle Obama

Michelle Obama is an American lawyer and former First Lady. As First Lady, she advocated for healthy living, education, and support for military families. She also initiated outreach to the African-American community and was a vocal advocate for women's rights.

Angelina Jolie

Angelina Jolie is an American actress and humanitarian. She has acted in a variety of films, but is perhaps best known for her roles in the Lara Croft films and Maleficent. She has also used her celebrity status to bring attention to the plight of refugees and has been an outspoken advocate for human rights.

Yuna Kim

Yuna Kim is a South Korean figure skater and Olympic champion. She is widely considered to be one of the most influential and successful female figure skaters of all time. Her success has inspired a generation of female skaters in South Korea and around the world.
